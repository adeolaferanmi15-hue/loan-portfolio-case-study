 The Story Behind the Analysis

Lending institutions grow when loans perform — but growth without risk control can quietly weaken a portfolio. This project explores how a loan portfolio is performing over time, where risks are concentrated, and what actions can support sustainable expansion.

Using cleaned loan data visualized in Looker Studio, the analysis transforms raw records into a clear picture of performance, behavior, and risk exposure.

 The Business Problem

As the portfolio expands, decision-makers need clarity on two things:

Is growth healthy and sustainable?

Where are the hidden pockets of risk that could threaten stability?

The objective was to evaluate portfolio performance and uncover patterns that influence repayment outcomes and credit risk distribution.

 Understanding the Data

The dataset contains loan records including borrower segments, loan categories, repayment status, interest income, and regional distribution.

Before analysis, the data was prepared in Excel to ensure reliability and consistency. This preparation stage ensured that the insights reflect real performance patterns rather than data quality issues.

Preparation Journey

.Removed duplicate records

.Addressed missing values

.Standardized formats across fields

.Converted dataset to CSV for visualization

With a clean foundation, the exploration began.

 Exploring the Portfolio

The dashboard was designed as a guided investigation across three analytical views — each answering a different business question.

⦁	Executive Overview — What is the overall health of the portfolio?

The portfolio demonstrates steady growth supported by consistent income generation. Most loans remain active, indicating strong engagement and repayment activity. However, performance is not uniform — risk levels vary across segments and regions, signaling the need for targeted monitoring.

This view establishes the central narrative: growth is strong, but risk distribution is uneven.

⦁	Portfolio Performance — What drives growth and stability?

Loan disbursement trends show consistent expansion, accompanied by rising interest income. Business loans contribute the largest share of portfolio value, positioning them as a primary growth driver. Education loans, meanwhile, display lower risk exposure, highlighting them as stable lending segments.

The portfolio is expanding with momentum — but performance differs by loan type, shaping where strategic focus should lie.

⦁	Risk and Regional Analysis — Where are the vulnerabilities?

Risk concentration becomes clearer when viewed across segments and geography. Personal loans exhibit higher risk exposure, while regional patterns reveal variation in credit quality. Some regions maintain stronger repayment behavior, while others require closer monitoring.

This analysis reveals that risk is not widespread — it is concentrated and manageable when identified early.

 What the Data Suggests

The findings point toward actionable strategies for sustainable portfolio growth:

⦁	Strengthen credit assessment for higher-risk loan categories

⦁	Implement region-specific risk monitoring approaches

⦁	Expand lending in stable, lower-risk segments

⦁	Enhance early warning systems to detect potential defaults

These actions balance growth with resilience.

 Final Reflection

The loan portfolio demonstrates healthy expansion supported by active participation and growing income generation. With targeted risk management and strategic lending decisions, the institution can reinforce long-term stability while continuing to grow responsibly.
